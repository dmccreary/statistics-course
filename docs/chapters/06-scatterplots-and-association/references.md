# References for Scatterplots and Association

Curated resources to deepen your understanding of scatterplots, correlation, and bivariate relationships.

---

## Wikipedia Articles

1. [Scatter plot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scatter_plot) - Wikipedia - Overview of scatterplot construction, interpretation, and common patterns including how to identify direction, form, and strength of associations.

2. [Correlation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correlation) - Wikipedia - Explains the concept of statistical correlation, different types of correlation measures, and the important distinction between correlation and causation.

3. [Pearson correlation coefficient](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pearson_correlation_coefficient) - Wikipedia - Detailed explanation of the correlation coefficient r, including its formula, properties, and interpretation guidelines for different values.

## Textbooks

4. **The Practice of Statistics** by Starnes, Tabor, Yates, and Moore - W.H. Freeman (2018) - Thorough treatment of scatterplots, correlation, and bivariate data analysis with AP exam-aligned examples and practice problems.

5. **Statistics in Action: Understanding a World of Data** by Watkins, Scheaffer, and Cobb - Key Curriculum Press (2008) - Activity-based approach to learning about association between variables with hands-on data exploration.

## Online Resources

6. **Correlation and Scatterplots** - Khan Academy - Video tutorials explaining how to create and interpret scatterplots, calculate correlation, and understand the properties and limitations of r.

7. **Guess the Correlation Game** - guessthecorrelation.com - Interactive game that helps students develop intuition for estimating correlation values by looking at scatterplots.

8. **NCTM Illuminations: Line of Best Fit** - National Council of Teachers of Mathematics - Interactive applet allowing students to explore how data points affect the correlation coefficient and best-fit line.

9. **Spurious Correlations** - tylervigen.com - Entertaining collection of absurd correlations demonstrating why correlation does not imply causation, perfect for classroom discussion.

10. **AP Statistics: Exploring Two-Variable Data** - College Board - Official AP course materials and sample questions covering scatterplots, correlation, and their proper interpretation on the exam.
