# References for Experimental Design

Curated resources to deepen your understanding of experimental design principles including control, randomization, replication, and blinding.

---

## Wikipedia Articles

1. [Design of experiments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Design_of_experiments) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of experimental design methodology, covering the historical development from R.A. Fisher's agricultural experiments to modern applications.

2. [Randomized controlled trial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randomized_controlled_trial) - Wikipedia - Explains why random assignment is essential for establishing causation, with examples from medical research showing how RCTs have transformed our understanding of treatments.

3. [Blind experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blind_experiment) - Wikipedia - Covers single-blind, double-blind, and even triple-blind experimental designs, explaining how blinding prevents expectation bias from contaminating results.

## Textbooks

4. **The Practice of Statistics** by Daren S. Starnes, Josh Tabor, and Dan Yates - W.H. Freeman (2018) - AP-aligned coverage of experimental design with detailed explanations of completely randomized, randomized block, and matched pairs designs, plus extensive practice problems.

5. **Design and Analysis of Experiments** by Douglas C. Montgomery - Wiley (2017) - The standard university textbook on experimental design, providing deeper mathematical foundations for those wanting to understand the theory behind good experiments.

## Online Resources

6. **Khan Academy: Designing Experiments** - Khan Academy - Video lessons explaining the difference between observational studies and experiments, with practice identifying treatments, factors, levels, and control groups.

7. **Stat Trek: Experimental Design** - Stat Trek - Clear tutorials on experimental design principles with step-by-step examples of how to set up completely randomized and blocked experiments.

8. **Understanding Science: How Science Works** - UC Berkeley - Interactive website explaining how controlled experiments fit into the broader scientific method, with examples showing how scientists design studies to test hypotheses.

9. **Against All Odds: Inside Statistics (Unit 11-12)** - Annenberg Learner - Video episodes showing real experimental designs in action, from agricultural field trials to medical studies testing new treatments.

10. **Cochrane Library: Introduction to Study Design** - Cochrane Collaboration - Overview of experimental study designs used in medical research, explaining why randomized controlled trials are considered the gold standard for establishing treatment effectiveness.
