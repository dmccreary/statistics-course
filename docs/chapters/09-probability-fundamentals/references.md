# References for Probability Fundamentals

Curated resources to deepen your understanding of probability theory, sample spaces, events, and the fundamental rules that govern chance.

---

## Wikipedia Articles

1. [Probability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probability) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of probability as a measure of the likelihood of events, covering theoretical, empirical, and subjective interpretations that align with the chapter's three probability perspectives.

2. [Sample space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sample_space) - Wikipedia - Detailed explanation of sample spaces as the set of all possible outcomes in probability experiments, with examples from dice rolls, coin flips, and card draws.

3. [Event (probability theory)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Event_(probability_theory)) - Wikipedia - Explains how events are subsets of sample spaces, covering simple events, compound events, and relationships between events including complements and intersections.

## Textbooks

4. **The Practice of Statistics** by Daren S. Starnes, Josh Tabor, and Dan Yates - W.H. Freeman (2018) - The standard AP Statistics textbook with extensive probability coverage, practice problems aligned to the AP curriculum, and clear explanations of the addition and multiplication rules.

5. **Statistics: Unlocking the Power of Data** by Robin H. Lock et al. - Wiley (2020) - Features excellent visual explanations of probability concepts with simulation-based approaches that reinforce the Law of Large Numbers through hands-on exploration.

## Online Resources

6. **Khan Academy: Probability** - Khan Academy - Free video lessons and practice exercises covering probability fundamentals from basic definitions through the multiplication rule, with immediate feedback on practice problems.

7. **Stat Trek: Probability Tutorial** - Stat Trek - Step-by-step tutorials explaining probability rules with worked examples, including interactive probability calculators for checking your work.

8. **Seeing Theory: Basic Probability** - Brown University - Beautiful interactive visualizations that let students explore sample spaces, Venn diagrams, and the Law of Large Numbers through dynamic simulations.

9. **AP Statistics Course and Exam Description** - College Board - Official guide outlining exactly what probability concepts are tested on the AP exam, including sample free-response questions with scoring guidelines.

10. **Against All Odds: Inside Statistics (Unit 18-20)** - Annenberg Learner - Video series produced for PBS that brings probability concepts to life through real-world applications in medicine, gambling, and weather forecasting.
