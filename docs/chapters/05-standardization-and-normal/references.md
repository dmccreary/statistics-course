# References for Standardization and Normal Distributions

Curated resources to deepen your understanding of z-scores, standardization, and the normal distribution.

---

## Wikipedia Articles

1. [Standard score](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_score) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive explanation of z-scores including the formula, properties, and applications in comparing values across different distributions.

2. [Normal distribution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Normal_distribution) - Wikipedia - Detailed coverage of the Gaussian distribution including its mathematical properties, parameters, and why it appears so frequently in nature and statistics.

3. [68-95-99.7 rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/68%E2%80%9395%E2%80%9399.7_rule) - Wikipedia - Explains the Empirical Rule for normal distributions, showing what percentage of data falls within one, two, and three standard deviations of the mean.

## Textbooks

4. **The Practice of Statistics** by Starnes, Tabor, Yates, and Moore - W.H. Freeman (2018) - The standard AP Statistics textbook with excellent coverage of normal distributions, z-scores, and probability calculations with real-world examples.

5. **Statistics: Learning from Data** by Roxy Peck and Tom Short - Cengage Learning (2018) - Clear explanations of standardization and normal curve applications with step-by-step examples ideal for high school students.

## Online Resources

6. **Normal Distribution Tutorial** - Khan Academy - Free video lessons walking through z-score calculations, using normal tables, and interpreting probabilities in context with practice problems.

7. **AP Statistics Course Description** - College Board - Official course framework explaining exactly what students need to know about normal distributions for the AP exam.

8. **StatKey: Normal Distribution Calculator** - Lock5stat.com - Interactive online tool for visualizing normal curves, calculating areas, and performing inverse normal calculations without tables.

9. **Seeing Theory: Normal Distribution** - Brown University - Beautiful interactive visualizations demonstrating how the normal curve relates to data distributions and probability.

10. **The Normal Distribution and Z-Scores** - Statistics by Jim - Accessible explanations with real-world examples showing how z-scores enable fair comparisons across different contexts and measurements.
