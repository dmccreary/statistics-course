# References for Communication and Synthesis

Curated resources to deepen your understanding of statistical communication, practical significance, and AP exam preparation strategies.

---

## Wikipedia Articles

1. [Statistical significance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_significance) - Wikipedia - Explains the difference between statistical and practical significance, the history of significance testing, and common misconceptions about p-values that students should understand.

2. [Effect size](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effect_size) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of effect size measures including Cohen's d, correlation coefficients, and guidelines for interpreting small, medium, and large effects in research contexts.

3. [Replication (statistics)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Replication_(statistics)) - Wikipedia - Discusses why replication matters in science, the reproducibility crisis, and how understanding study limitations helps interpret whether findings are trustworthy.

## Textbooks

4. **The Practice of Statistics** by Starnes, Tabor, Yates, and Moore - W.H. Freeman (2018) - Includes chapters on communicating statistical results, understanding study design limitations, and synthesizing inference procedures for comprehensive AP exam preparation.

5. **Statistics in Plain English** by Timothy Urdan - Routledge (2017) - Exceptionally clear explanations of statistical concepts written for students, with emphasis on interpretation and communication rather than computation.

## Online Resources

6. **AP Statistics Course and Exam Description** - College Board - Essential resource containing complete learning objectives, sample exam questions, scoring guidelines, and the official formula sheet students receive on exam day.

7. **Albert.io AP Statistics** - Albert.io - Practice questions organized by topic with detailed explanations, including free-response questions graded according to AP rubrics with feedback on common errors.

8. **StatsClass: AP Statistics Review** - StatsClass - Video review sessions covering all major topics with exam-taking strategies, time management tips, and practice with calculator procedures.

9. **How to Lie with Statistics** - Darrell Huff (Classic Text Summary) - Understanding this classic helps students recognize misleading statistics in media and develops critical thinking about data presentation and study limitations.

10. **ASA Statement on P-Values** - American Statistical Association - Official guidance from professional statisticians on proper interpretation of p-values and the importance of considering effect sizes and practical significance alongside statistical tests.
