## Lesson Plan

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this activity, students will be able to:

1. Recall essential formulas for AP Statistics
2. List the conditions required for various inference procedures
3. Identify key phrases that earn points on free response questions
4. Demonstrate proficiency with calculator functions
5. Avoid common errors that result in lost points

### Target Audience

- AP Statistics students preparing for the exam
- Statistics teachers creating review materials
- Students reviewing for cumulative assessments

### Prerequisites

- Completion of AP Statistics course content
- Basic familiarity with TI-84 calculator

### Classroom Activities

**Activity 1: Self-Assessment (20 minutes)**

1. Have students work through the checklist individually
2. Check only items they feel completely confident about
3. Identify their top 3 weakest areas
4. Create study plans focused on those areas

**Activity 2: Partner Quiz (15 minutes)**

Pair students up:
1. One student reads an item from the checklist
2. Partner explains it or demonstrates it
3. If correct, check the item
4. Switch roles

**Activity 3: Formula Flashcards (10 minutes)**

Use the Essential Formulas section to create flashcards:
- Formula on one side
- When to use it + example on the other

*"Don't worry—every statistician drops an acorn sometimes,"* Sylvia reassures. *"The goal isn't perfection on day one. It's making progress every day until exam day!"*

### Assessment Questions

1. Write the formula for a confidence interval. What are the three components?

2. What are the three conditions you must check before doing inference? Give an example of how to verify each.

3. Explain why you should never say "accept the null hypothesis."

4. On your TI-84, what function would you use to find P(Z < 1.5)?

5. A student concludes "The data is significant at the 0.05 level." What's wrong with this conclusion, and how should it be fixed?

## Study Tips from Sylvia

*"Here are my top tips for exam success,"* Sylvia shares:

1. **Practice the four-step process** until it's automatic
2. **Always conclude in context** - this is where many students lose easy points
3. **Know your calculator functions** - they save time and reduce errors
4. **Check conditions FIRST** - before doing any calculations
5. **Review common mistakes** the night before - fresh reminders help!
